Introduction

Good evening! Please take up a chair; I will bring you some sparkling water in a moment. Feel free to browse.

This is the website of me, Alan Rempel. I am a student at the University of King's College (Halifax, NS). I am fond of tea and wool sweaters. I like programming in Haskell, especially when I have no spare time in which to do so. You may find me on Instagram as @alan_rempel_mdash and on GitHub as @acerempel.

Currently this website consists of my blog, which I update according as I have spare time and whim, and some miscellaneous lists.

The only reason this website exists is that I find it enjoyable to work on it in my spare time (and even in time I have not to spare!). I also like experimenting with alternatives to the usual ways of presenting oneself in writing on the Internet. I have nothing against social media, though; I even post on Instagram occasionally.

Why “three dots”? Because three dots is the visual form of the ellipsis (‘…’), and I am often an elliptical man.

Concerning the blog: I post highly irregularly. Posts are dated by when I originally composed their content. This date may be months or years earlier than when the post appears here—I often post backdated items that I had written as a note to myself much earlier. In this sense, the blog is a sort of artificial construct: well—it is highly curated.

There is no commenting facility (yet), but if you send me an email commenting on a post, I might, with your permission post your comment below the post.

I am constantly intermittently adding, editing, removing stuff, adding stuff back again, and tweaking the design of the site, so check back again soon!

I hope your find this website an amusing stop on your internet travels.

Yours,
Alan
