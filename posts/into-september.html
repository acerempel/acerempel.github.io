Notes for the Fall of 4th Year

The Shape Note Choir.

Alan has to be absolutely on top of the Shape Note Choir right away. For one thing, leading the Shape Note Choir means he will be responsible for part of the liturgy in the Friday Evensong. For another, how fun and meaningful folks find shape note singing at the Chapel will depend in part on the quality of his direction and management of the choir. For a third, this is a position constitutes an opportunity to him to be excellent. Go beyond obligation and explore things like possible collaboration with other a cappella groups in Halifax, Harold Rennie and friends. (Apparently he knows Sandra Bender! Not really a surprise.) Or a trip out to Sackville---Fr. Ingalls mentioned he knows an expert on shape notes out there. I'd advise him to take it up as his own and make it something he can be proud of.

Ancient Greek pedagogy.

I don't know specifically. Keep hacking away at it. Talk with Eli, Matthew, Grundke maybe. So far this is just a concept of a personal project for him, not yet even an actual project, and certainly not a responsibility (maybe he could see if he can't make it into one? But who knows whether that will make any sense --- see how it rolls). Keep reading---both secondary material, linguistic and pedagogical, and actual ancient greek texts!---throughout the summer, and we'll see where we're at come September. I suppose he'd better take Intermediate Greek in the fall --- will be too bad about John of Damascus, but the time for the church fathers will come --- Greek is actually necessary for his degree at the moment, besides being of greater special interest to him.

Household finances.

Another gig that Alan can take up as his own and be excellent in.

Haskell scripts to help with budgeting, figuring out who owes what to who, applying a budget template for the week. Automate or make it easy to turn the ledger file(s) into a spreadsheet, charts. Alan could do this in Excel or, alternatively, serialize the table/chart data and make the chart programmatically, either in Haskell (which would mean we could use hledger-lib directly, he wouldn't actually have to serialize anything) or PureScript/Elm.

But also: habits. Reminding cats to put cash in the box. Keeping careful track of who puts how much in each week, filing receipts straightaway, etc. I should remind him to make sure every week that the records are complete and accurate---which will often involve asking cats how much they spent on x, or whether they put cash in the box this week!

Fun!

I know he really enjoyed watching Scream Queens with Emma. Maybe he could arrange to have a tv-watching friend again.
